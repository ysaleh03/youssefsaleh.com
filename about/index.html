This page contains more information on what I do, as well as some contact information if you want to reach out.

university

I am currently in the fourth year of my BSc. with Honours in Computer Science at the University of British Columbia.

I'm also an Undergraduate Teaching Assistant for CPSC 320 (Intermediate Algorithm Design and Analysis). In the past, I've been a TA for CPSC 210 (Software Construction) and CPSC 312 (Functional and Logic Programming). To read more about my TA experience, check out my department page!

Some of my favourite courses so far have been CPSC 311, CPSC 312, CPSC 320, CPSC 411, CPSC 420 and CPSC 421.

research

I'm currently working on finding research in formal verification.

In 2024, I did a directed study with Professor Mark Greenstreet on the topic — more specifically I worked on proving the equivalence of depth first search on Huffman trees and its tail-recursive version using ACL2. You can read the report here.

interests

My academic interests lie broadly in theoretical computer science, with a particular focus on programming languages, formal verification and theory of computation.

For the past year, I've been familiarizing myself with proof assistants like ACL2 and Lean. I am currently teaching myself Viper and Rocq.

When it comes to programming paradigms, I vastly prefer functional. Most of my (functional) experience is with Haskell and Racket, though a few months with Common Lisp have convinced me I'd enjoy most lisps. I am currently learning Rust — multiparadigm as it is.

Beyond that, I enjoy going down Wikipedia rabbit holes as much as the next person, especially when they're about historical linguistics.
